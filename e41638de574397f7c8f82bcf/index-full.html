Common Terms Glossary

Understanding key marketing and SEO metrics in plain English

Traffic Metrics

Organic Traffic

Free visitors who find your website through search engines (Google, Bing) without you paying for ads. Higher = better SEO.

Paid Traffic

Visitors who click on your paid advertisements (Google Ads, Facebook Ads). You pay per click or impression.

Branded Traffic

Visitors searching specifically for your brand name (e.g., "Luce Divina candles"). Indicates brand awareness.

Traffic Share

Your percentage of total traffic in a market compared to competitors. 55% means you get more than half of the available visitors.

SEO & Authority

Authority Score

A 0-100 score measuring your website's overall strength and trustworthiness. Higher scores mean search engines trust you more. 40+ is good, 60+ is excellent.

Referring Domains (Ref. Domains)

Number of unique websites linking to you. Like recommendations - more diverse sites linking = more trust from Google.

Backlinks

Total number of links pointing to your site from other websites. Quality matters more than quantity. Good backlinks boost rankings.

SEO Value

The estimated dollar amount you'd have to spend on ads to get the same traffic you're getting for free from organic search.

Keywords

Organic Keywords

Search terms your website ranks for in Google without paying. More keywords = more ways people can find you.

Paid Keywords

Search terms you're bidding on in Google Ads. You pay when someone searches these and clicks your ad.

CPC (Cost Per Click)

How much you pay each time someone clicks your ad. Higher CPC = more competitive/valuable keyword. $5 CPC means $5 per click.

AI & Visibility

AI Visibility

How often your brand appears in AI tools like ChatGPT, Google AI Overview, and Gemini. The new frontier of search visibility.

Mentions

Times your brand is referenced across the web in articles, social media, forums, etc. More mentions = more brand awareness.

Cited Pages

Your website pages that AI tools reference when answering questions. Being cited means AI trusts your content.

Marketing Strategy

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)

Revenue earned for every $1 spent on ads. ROAS of 3x means you make $3 for every $1 spent. 2x+ is typically profitable.

CPC (Cost Per Click)

How much you pay each time someone clicks your ad. Lower CPC = more efficient ad spend. Industry average for candles is ~$1.23.

CPP (Cost Per Purchase)

Total ad spend divided by number of purchases. Shows how much it costs to acquire a sale. Lower is better. Candle industry median is ~$29.

CTR (Click-Through Rate)

Percentage of people who click your ad after seeing it. Calculated as clicks ÷ impressions × 100. Higher CTR = more compelling ad. 2%+ is good.

AOV (Average Order Value)

Average amount spent per order. Calculated as total revenue ÷ number of orders. Higher AOV = more revenue per customer. Luxury candles typically $50-150.

LTV (Lifetime Value)

Total revenue expected from a customer over their entire relationship with your brand. Higher LTV justifies higher acquisition costs. LTV:CAC ratio of 3:1+ is healthy.

Attribution

Figuring out which marketing channels (ads, email, social) actually led to a sale. Helps you know where to invest your budget.

Full-Funnel Marketing

Marketing strategy covering all stages: TOFU (awareness), MOFU (consideration), BOFU (purchase). Builds long-term customer relationships.
